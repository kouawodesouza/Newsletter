This week the AI story split in two directions at once: frontier labs kept shipping — Google, OpenAI, xAI and China's DeepSeek and Alibaba all pushed out new models — while Europe's AI Act finally grew teeth, with today, July 22, the deadline for companies to sign onto its Code of Practice. Meanwhile France's Mistral leaned harder into Microsoft's cloud and China's Tencent circled Manus. The pace of releases isn't slowing, but the rules and the ownership structures around AI are catching up fast.

— Coach Fredo

THIS WEEK AT A GLANCE

  • Google released three new Gemini models, including a cybersecurity-focused variant limited to government partners. (TechCrunch)
  • GPT-5.6's Sol/Terra/Luna tiers finished rolling out to all ChatGPT plans and landed on AWS Bedrock. (Axios)
  • Mistral signed a multibillion-dollar Microsoft deal to expand its French data centers and reach U.S. Azure customers. (France 24)
  • DeepSeek's V4 model hit general availability at a fraction of U.S. pricing, and Alibaba shipped its 2.4-trillion-parameter Qwen 3.8. (Bloomberg)
  • Today, July 22, is the deadline for AI firms to sign the EU's Code of Practice ahead of August enforcement. (Tech Times)
  • Musk said Grok 4.6's training run wraps this week, following Grok 4.5's frontier debut and an open-sourced coding agent. (Dataconomy)
THE BIG SIX

OpenAI / ChatGPT

OpenAI finished rolling out GPT-5.6 across all ChatGPT plans this week in three tiers — Sol (flagship, with a new Ultra subagent mode), Terra (mid-tier), and Luna (fast/cheap) — and the models are now live on Amazon Bedrock. OpenAI is also retiring its Atlas browser agent by August 9 as those capabilities fold directly into ChatGPT and Codex.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: If you're on a paid ChatGPT plan, you already have GPT-5.6 — try Terra first for everyday tasks; it targets GPT-5.5-level quality at roughly half the cost.
Source: Axios, July 9, 2026

Google / Gemini

Google DeepMind released three new models on July 21: Gemini 3.6 Flash (its workhorse model, cutting token usage by up to 17%), 3.5 Flash-Lite (its cheapest tier), and 3.5 Flash Cyber, a cybersecurity-tuned model available only to governments and trusted partners in a limited pilot.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: Gemini 3.6 Flash's lower token usage means cheaper API bills if you're already building on Google's models — worth a re-check of your usage costs this month.
Source: TechCrunch, July 21, 2026

Anthropic / Claude

Anthropic rebuilt Claude's memory system this month, replacing the old daily summary with individual, categorized entries Claude reads and updates as you talk. Separately, boosted Claude Code weekly rate limits and Fable 5 access on Max/Team Premium plans were both extended through mid-to-late August.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: The new memory system means Claude retains more useful detail about your business across sessions — worth revisiting if you gave up on it for forgetting context before.
Source: Digital Applied, July 12, 2026

Manus AI

Tencent remains in talks to become Manus AI's largest shareholder, stepping in as alternative investors seek to buy the company back from Meta for at least $2 billion after Beijing ordered Meta's original acquisition unwound this spring. The deal was still pending as of this week.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: Manus remains usable for agentic task automation regardless of the ownership shuffle — no action needed unless you're deep in a long-term integration.
Source: Bloomberg, July 10, 2026

Microsoft / Copilot & Azure AI

Microsoft made GPT-5.6 the default model across Microsoft 365 Copilot (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Chat) even as analysts flag growing distance between Microsoft and OpenAI. Microsoft's own $2.5B "Frontier Company" unit, launched earlier this month, is now helping enterprises deploy AI with more vendor independence.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: If you're on Microsoft 365, Copilot is now running on OpenAI's newest model by default — no setup needed, but it's worth testing whether it changes your workflows.
Source: TechCrunch, July 9, 2026

xAI / Grok

Elon Musk said xAI's next model, Grok 4.6, was set to finish its initial training run this week, following Grok 4.5's July 8 launch. xAI also open-sourced its coding agent, "Grok Build," on GitHub and rolled out Automations, letting users schedule jobs or trigger them from matching emails.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: Grok's new Automations feature is worth a look if you want simple if-this-then-that style workflows without building your own integration.
Source: Dataconomy, July 20, 2026
INTERNATIONAL

🇫🇷 France

Mistral AI struck a multibillion-dollar deal with Microsoft on July 21 to expand Azure infrastructure inside Mistral's own French data centers and bring Mistral Medium 3.5 and OCR 4 to Azure Foundry for U.S. customers — a notable tie-up for a company that has otherwise pushed hard for European AI independence.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: European SMBs get an easier on-ramp to Mistral's models through Azure — useful if data residency in the EU matters for your compliance obligations.
Source: France 24, July 21, 2026

🇪🇺 European Union

Today, July 22, is the deadline for AI companies to submit a signatory form for the EU's Code of Practice — signing confers a presumption of regulatory compliance. Article 50 chatbot-disclosure rules are already in force, and the European Commission gains the power to fine general-purpose AI providers starting August 2.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: If you serve EU customers and use AI chatbots or generated content, check your disclosure labeling now — the Commission's fining power starts in under two weeks.
Source: Tech Times, July 21, 2026

🇨🇳 China

DeepSeek's V4 model reached general availability on July 19, priced as low as 87 cents per million tokens during off-peak hours — a fraction of Western frontier pricing. Alibaba shipped Qwen 3.8, a 2.4-trillion-parameter open-weight model, and Apple approved both Alibaba's and Baidu's tech for Apple Intelligence in China.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR SMBs: Cheap, capable open-weight models out of China are trickling into more products — worth watching if API cost is a bigger constraint for you than brand-name model choice.
Source: Bloomberg, July 17, 2026

PRACTICAL TAKEAWAY

Two things are worth 20 minutes of your time this week. First, if you serve any customers in the EU, check that your chatbots and AI-generated content are properly disclosed — enforcement money starts flowing August 2. Second, if you're paying per-token for AI anywhere in your stack, compare your current rate against what Gemini 3.6 Flash and DeepSeek V4 now charge — prices keep dropping and it's an easy line-item to trim.
